NbMeta

The notebook metadata dilema

August 20, 2018

The origins of this project are began one July evening in Tuscon, AZ at the 2018 ESIP Summer Meeting, when we got together to explore a FUNding Friday project proposal. We talked about some of the issues around the proliferation of notebooks and how this problem is only getting worse.

We worked over some beverages and interactions with others and decided to pitch … yielding this:

Here are the fundamental questions driving our curiousity and now funded efforts:

  • Notebooks are proliferating in places like Github, but finding notebooks of value is becoming more difficult as a result. So, What can we do to first understand how bad the problem is?
  • Finding notebooks is a metadata problem. Finding notebooks of value is a curation problem. Thus, How can we extract (perhaps in an automated way) metadata to support findability and curation?
  • Making notebooks searchable therefore requires technologies to support a metadata solution and a search solution (e.g. a stack to support the efforts put into the metadata). So, What are the important metadata technologies (JSON-LD, Codemeta, etc.) the we will need to craft a solution?

    Also, What are the technology supports necessary to store, retrieve and update the metadata we do extract?

This is how we have come to this point of exploration – we believe this is a journey well worth taking, since the importance of notebooks everywhere (i.e. education, industry, etc.) is on track to continue growing into the foreseeable future.
